Popular Destinations

  1. Algiers

    Algiers is the capital city of Algeria and is located on the Mediterranean coast. The city has a rich history, with its Old Town, known as the Casbah, being designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Algiers is also home to a number of museums and galleries, including the National Museum of Fine Arts and the National Museum of Antiquities.

  2. Oran

    Oran is a coastal city in western Algeria, known for its colonial architecture and vibrant music scene. The city has a number of historic landmarks, including the Saint Eugene Cathedral and the La Blanca Mosque. Oran is also famous for its annual International Music Festival, which showcases traditional and modern music from around the world.

  3. Constantine

    Constantine is a city located in the northeast of Algeria and is known for its scenic surroundings and historic landmarks. The city is built on a plateau and is surrounded by deep valleys and gorges, making it a popular destination for outdoor activities like hiking and rock climbing. Constantine is also home to a number of historic buildings and monuments, including the Hadj Lakhdar Mosque and the Pont Sidi Rached.

  4. Annaba

    Annaba is a city located in the northeast of Algeria and is known for its beaches and ancient Roman ruins. The city is home to the Roman theater, which dates back to the 2nd century AD and is one of the largest in the country. Annaba is also a popular destination for foodies, with a number of traditional Algerian restaurants serving up delicious local cuisine.

  5. Blida

    Blida is a city located in the hills of Algeria and is known for its scenic surroundings and historic landmarks. The city is home to the Château de Blida, a 19th-century castle that now serves as a museum, as well as a number of gardens and parks, including the Jardin d'Essai du Hamma.

  6. Béjaïa

    Béjaïa is a coastal city in northeast Algeria and is known for its beaches and vibrant cultural scene. The city is home to the Béjaïa Museum, which showcases the region's history and cultural heritage, as well as a number of historic landmarks, including the Kasbah of Béjaïa and the Great Mosque of Béjaïa.

  7. Tlemcen

    Tlemcen is a city located in western Algeria and is known for its rich history and cultural heritage. The city is home to a number of historic landmarks, including the Great Mosque of Tlemcen and the Dar Batha Museum, which showcases the city's traditional arts and crafts. Tlemcen is also famous for its annual International Festival of World Sacred Music, which brings together musicians from around the world to celebrate sacred music.

  8. Sétif

    Sétif is a city located in northeastern Algeria and is known for its scenic surroundings and historic landmarks. The city is home to the Sétif Museum, which showcases the region's history and cultural heritage, as well as a number of historic buildings and monuments, including the Great Mosque of Sétif and the Sétif Fort.

  9. Batna

    Batna is a city located in northeast Algeria and is known for its scenic surroundings and historic landmarks. The city is home to a number of historic buildings and monuments, including the Batna Fort and the Batna Museum, which showcases the region's history and cultural heritage.

Historical Events

  1. Independence from France

    Date: July 5, 1962

    Algeria gained independence from France on July 5, 1962, after a long and bloody struggle for freedom and independence. The independence movement was led by the National Liberation Front and resulted in the country becoming a sovereign nation, free from colonial rule.

  2. The Algerian War of Independence

    Date: November 1, 1954 - July 5, 1962

    The Algerian War of Independence was a conflict between France and Algerian independence fighters that lasted from 1954 to 1962. The war resulted in the independence of Algeria and marked a major turning point in the country's history.

  3. Algerian Revolution

    Date: November 1954 - July 1962

    The Algerian Revolution was a political and social revolution that took place in Algeria from 1954 to 1962. The revolution was a response to the oppression and exploitation of the Algerian people by the French colonial government, and resulted in the independence of Algeria and the establishment of a new government.

  4. Green March

    Date: November 6, 1975

    The Green March was a peaceful demonstration held in 1975 in which hundreds of thousands of Algerians marched to the Western Sahara to claim their sovereignty over the territory. The march was a significant moment in Algeria's modern history and demonstrated the country's commitment to self-determination and independence.

  5. Algerian Civil War

    Date: 1991-2002

    The Algerian Civil War was a conflict that took place from 1991 to 2002, and was the result of political and social tensions within the country. The conflict resulted in significant loss of life and displacement, and had a lasting impact on the country's political and social landscape.
